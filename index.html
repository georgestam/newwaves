About me

I born in Barcelona but now live in London. I have been always passionate about tech and software, perhaps because I grew up with a ZX Spectrum 48k (oh yes, 48KB!) in the 80s and a Commodore Amiga 500 in the 90s. Despite my love for software and innovation, I somehow ended working in one of the oldest (and less dynamic) engineering disciplines (Mechanical Engineering).

Therefore, after a career spanning over 15 years as a Chartered Mechanical and Lead Subsea Engineer in multimillion deep-water projects within Oil & Gas (yes, it sounds cool but is also boring), I decided to make a career change into our amazing-human-created-deep-space that we call The Internet. I enrolled in an intensive 9 week Ruby on Rails programme in Le Wagon on summer 2016 and I work as a happy Web Developer since then.

I decided to build as a minimum 12 projects in 12 months inspired from Pieter Levels and Mr Rajagopalan. Mr Rajagopalan advice is "build, build, build, test and then plan" (contrary of what always I did!). I do this mainly to improve my skills and get better jobs as a Web Developer.

I also write about this third-time-life-adventure-of-career-change in my blog. I do hope to be helping others following similar (tough and fun) path.
